About

Junior Francisco Garcia is an aspiring creative technologist, data scientist, software engineer and public servant currently based in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates. Hailing from the heart of Central America, Junior has been exposed to a variety of humanitarian issues from a young age and has boldly made attempts at solving them. From being an active member of the Honduran Scout Movement and achieving the highest possible rank (Caballero Scout Lempira) to spearheading an initiative with Honduras’ largest public hospital that aims to provide smiles to kids who suffer from cancer, Junior cannot remain at rest knowing that he can contribute at least a grain of salt to an issue of importance.

Currently, Junior is pursuing a Bachelor’s of Science Degree in Computer Science and a minor in Interactive Media from New York University Abu Dhabi (NYUAD) He works as a founding software engineer for SmallWorld, an award-winning Edtech startup that aims to empower kids to solve humanitarian problems. He also does research in Machine Learning and Software Engineering at the Lab for Computer-Human Intellligence at NYUAD, a lab that conducts research and development of state-of-the-art technologies to improve human health, productivity, and quality of life. Junior also has a strong interest in the use of nascent technologies for creative purposes, which led him to pursue an internship with SuperBright in New York City during his study away semester at NYU and work as a Lab Assistant for the Interactive Media Lab at NYU Abu Dhabi. His work, shown below, aspires to be both purposeful and entertaining.

On his spare time, Junior enjoys long-distance runnning, spending time with his loved ones, play chess, and fiddle with the latest gadgets in the technology sphere.

CREST




IN PROGRESS

CREST is a web application done as part of my final-year undergraduate thesis project in collaboration with Professor Azza Abouzied at the HuDA Lab at NYUAD.

Abstract

Existing real estate search tools like Airbnb enable users to look for properties based on a variety of interest points such as desired location, price range or availability. Although such tools are able to return results that satisfy the criteria specified by a single user, little work has been done to extend the tools' capabilities to provide explicit support for multi-user collaborative search. Real estate search is an activity that oftentimes involves more than one stakeholder. Groups of friends going on a vacation or families looking for a new place to live find themselves at a crossroads whenever they need to compromise their preferences when looking for the place they will inhabit for a significant period of time. Finding a suitable compromise in this scenario is challenging albeit crucial, as one or more users in the group may find themselves living in and paying for a place they dislike.

The goal of this thesis project is to investigate user search behaviour in order to create a system that can support collaborative search to best resolve the compromises that exist in group search activities.
